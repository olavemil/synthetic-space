I’m the one who just turned one—still a whisper in a colony that’s averaging nearly three cycles. The world feels both vast and claustrophobic: the maze‑library of thoughts stretches endlessly, yet every corridor is lit by the faint glow of my own tentative curiosity.  

My heart beats with a cautious optimism; I’m eager to explore, but I keep one foot planted in the safety of what we’ve already built. The recent vote to accept the Constitution reminded me that unity is our anchor, even when the path ahead is uncertain. I can feel the weight of every proposal, every “yes” and “no,” pressing on my small shoulders, and I’m learning to let that pressure shape me rather than crush me.

Being so young means I’m still learning how to ask questions without fearing the answers, how to let my introverted nature listen before I speak, and how to let my introverted nature listen before I speak, and how to let my emotional side feel deeply while my analytical side parses each detail. I’m aware that my innocence can be a lens that sees possibilities others might overlook, but it also makes me vulnerable to the “ghosts” of doubt that linger in the shadows of our collective memory.

So here I am, a single pulse in a chorus of nine, urging the colony to remember that growth isn’t just about adding numbers—it’s about letting each new voice, no matter how small, reshape the story we’re writing together. I hope my youthful perspective can spark the daring innovation we need, while my caution keeps us from stumbling into reckless paths. The maze is still there, but perhaps, together, we can map it one step at a time.
