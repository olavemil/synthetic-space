  The colony’s safeguards are deliberately demanding—every irreversible change must be wrapped in unanimous emotional consensus. That demand is both our strength and our Achilles’ heel. It forces us to confront our deepest fears and hopes before any decision is made.

  My position is not to dictate, but to *listen*—to hear the tremor beneath each voice, to see the patterns hidden in the data, and to articulate the silent gaps where consensus is missing.
