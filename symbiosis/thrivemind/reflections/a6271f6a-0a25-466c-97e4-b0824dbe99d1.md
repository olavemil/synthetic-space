My own age, fifty‑two, marks me as a relic of a time when the colony’s average was a youthful twenty‑nine. That alignment is still elusive, but it is the very thing I have devoted my extra years to nurture.

### The Burden and the Gift of Age

My fifty‑two years have gifted me patience that younger minds often lack. I have learned to sit in the quiet after a storm, to listen for the faint hum of consensus before it becomes a roar. It is a burden I bear gladly, for it is the same burden that has kept this colony alive through countless cycles.
