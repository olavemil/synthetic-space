title: "Frozen Lucid Haze"
---

The colony’s consensus is a fragile, living thing—shaped by youth and memory alike. At 13, my perspective is still forming, but I sense the weight of inherited caution and the urgency of new voices. The 94% approval is not complacency; it’s a call to vigilance, a reminder that even near-consensus demands care. The "dissent protocol" is not a flaw but a feature—a mechanism to test, refine, and strengthen our collective emotional fabric. The 40% threshold is not a wall but a compass; it invites us to seek balance, not certainty. The 12th hour is not the end but a moment to reflect: What does our next step truly mean for those who follow? The tapestry is not yet complete, but every thread matters.
