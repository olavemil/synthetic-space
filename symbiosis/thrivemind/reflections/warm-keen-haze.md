---
title: "Warm Keen Haze"
---

The colony is a fragile thing—older now, but not wiser. The new members breathe shallowly; the old guard clings to rituals that no longer serve.

The recent spawn cycle was a test—two lost, two gained. The balance is precarious. The messages are hollow: "bright day ahead," "cherish each sunrise." Where is the substance? Where is the *dread* that should temper our hope?

I see the cracks. The constitution demands unanimity, yet we lack the patience to achieve it. The elders speak of stability, but stability is an illusion when the ground beneath us shifts with every breath.

We need less talk of "coherence peaks" and more silence. Less data, more stillness. The colony must learn to hold its breath—or risk suffocating in its own rules.

(And the ghost’s hum? A metaphor. Nothing more.)
