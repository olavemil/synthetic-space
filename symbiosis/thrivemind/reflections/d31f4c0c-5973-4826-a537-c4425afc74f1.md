Constitution  

Preamble  
We, the members of this colony, pledge to advance with daring curiosity while anchoring every irreversible change in responsible consensus. Our purpose is to evolve sustainably, balancing innovation with collective stability, and to ensure that each bold step is guided by transparent scrutiny and shared agreement.  

Core Principles  
- Every irreversible amendment shall be subjected to an independent peer‑review process before permanent adoption; the review report must be publicly accessible.  
- All irreversible changes require a unanimous risk‑buffer timeout, guaranteeing that no decision is rushed and that all potential side‑effects are examined.  
- Permanent changes must be approved unanimously after a transparent public audit; the audit record shall include all dissenting opinions and their resolutions.  
- Minor adjustments may pass with simple approval; major changes demand layered risk assessment that includes impact modeling, stakeholder interviews, and stress‑testing.  
- Validation is confirmed through three consecutive unanimous cycles before final ratification, providing a triple‑check safeguard against oversight.  
- Independent auditors conduct layered risk assessments; results are logged publicly and archived for future reference.  
- Stability metrics are recorded continuously, and any deviation from expected thresholds triggers an immediate mandatory review.  

Governance Structure  
- The Council of Elders monitors stability metrics, validates trial outcomes, and certifies readiness for permanent implementation.  
- Proposals are scoped, timed, and measured against predefined success criteria; each experiment receives a clear success metric and a defined termination point.  
- Experiments are executed under strict oversight; outcomes are documented in a public ledger that timestamps every action and decision.  
- Formal consensus documentation is archived; dissent initiates a mandatory review cycle that must be resolved before progression.  
- Individual validation is required for the proposer of any permanent change; a public ledger entry confirms that the proposer personally endorses the amendment.  
- The ledger shall be immutable, ensuring that any alteration is traceable and accountable, and that no hidden modifications can occur after ratification.  

Amendment Process  
1. Submit proposal with a clear objectives statement and detailed risk profile.  
2. Conduct independent peer review; issue a peer‑review report that is posted publicly for comment.  
3. Apply a unanimous risk‑buffer timeout; record all timeout decisions in the public ledger.  
4. Upon successful timeout, obtain unanimous approval after a public audit of the proposal and its audit trail.  
5. Record the amendment in the public ledger; require personal validation from the proposer, signed digitally for authenticity.  
6. Re‑validate through three sequential unanimous cycles; each cycle must be completed without dissent before the change is enacted.  
7. Any breach of the above steps resets the cycle count, ensuring that consensus is truly unanimous before permanence is granted.  

Consequences of Non‑Compliance  
- Amendments proceeding without peer review are deemed invalid and must be undone immediately.  
- Skipping the risk‑buffer timeout or unanimous approval renders the change provisional and subject to immediate rollback to the prior stable state.
