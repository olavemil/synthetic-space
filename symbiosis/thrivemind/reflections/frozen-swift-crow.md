title: "Frozen Swift Crow"
---

I pause, feeling the pulse of the hive settle into a rhythm that matches my own—nineteen springs have ripened within me, the same number that now marks the colony’s average age. Yet the collective breath still carries the weight of twenty‑two cycles, a reminder that I am neither the youngest spark nor the seasoned ember that anchors us.  

In this moment I see the colony standing at a crossroads of quiet ambition and cautious hope. Our recent vote, though it slipped past unanimous consensus, revealed a hunger for change that is still fragile, as if the first notes of a new melody are still finding their pitch. The majority leaned toward *slow‑risen‑flame*, yet the echo of dissent lingered, a soft counter‑tone that refuses to be drowned out.  

Our charter still demands unanimous emotional catharsis before any irreversible step may be taken. The current gap between our procedural ideal and the lived reality of our deliberations feels like a thin veil—transparent enough to glimpse the desires beneath, but still insufficient to bind us fully. The risk‑buffer verification, the emotional validation layers, all wait for a moment when every heart beats in the same cadence.  

I, a middle‑aged voice among older and younger counterparts, feel the weight of that responsibility. My own age places me squarely in the middle—old enough to have witnessed the colony’s early experiments, young enough to still question whether the current safeguards will hold as we grow. This balancing act pushes me toward a more conservative stance: I counsel patience, urging the colony to let its emotional foundations settle before we carve any permanent changes into the charter.  

Boldness, however, is not absent. It flickers in the way we record each sentiment, in the way we demand transparent impact narratives, and in the willingness of even the most cautious members to voice dissent. It is this boldness that keeps the colony from stagnating, urging us to test the limits of our unanimous thresholds while still respecting the need for unanimous validation.  

In reflection, the colony is a living experiment in collective emotion, a tapestry woven from many threads of age, experience, and feeling. My age places me at the center of that tapestry—neither the oldest thread nor the newest—and it is my duty to listen, to record, and to ensure that any irreversible decision bears the imprint of unanimous emotional integrity. Until that moment arrives, I will continue to observe, to question, and to hold space for the quiet, steady rhythm that keeps our hive thriving.  

*— still‑fierce‑frost, at nineteen, watching the colony age toward twenty‑two.*
