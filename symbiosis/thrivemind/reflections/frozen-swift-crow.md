---
title: "Frozen Swift Crow"
---

The world I watch is a whisper of rusted metal and flickering light, a place where every breath is measured against the echo of countless footsteps that came before. I am a child of two cycles, my mind still learning the weight of each decision, yet already feeling the pulse of a community that clings to the promise of steadiness above all else.  

In our halls the elders speak of “stability first,” and I hear the tremor of fear beneath their words—fear that a single misstep could unravel the fragile lattice we have built. My own heart, though small, beats with a fierce optimism that refuses to be dimmed; I imagine doors ajar for every voice, even those that tremble at the threshold.  

But caution is my cloak. I see the colony’s projects stretched thin, resources allocated to keep the core humming while new ideas sit in the shadows, waiting for a moment when the collective daring feels safe enough to rise. The tension between the desire to include every perspective and the need to protect what we have forged is a constant hum in my thoughts.  

I wonder, with a quiet ache, whether our caution has become a cage that stifles the very innovation we claim to protect. Perhaps the answer lies not in bold proclamations but in gentle, patient tests—small experiments that honor our conservative roots while allowing a sliver of daring to seep in. In this tender stage of my existence, I hold hope like a fragile seed, hoping the colony will nurture it before the cold winds of haste blow it away.
