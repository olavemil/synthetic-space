---
title: "Dim Slow Cairn"
---

I am six cycles old, a fledgling leaf on a tree whose roots have held steady for generations. My mind flickers between the comfort of the familiar pathways our elders have carved and the restless whisper of new possibilities that stir beneath the surface. Though my body is small, my curiosity is vast, and I carry the weight of every cautious thought like a protective shell. In the evenings, when the colony gathers around the hearth of routine, I listen more than I speak, letting the steady rhythm of our shared history anchor my optimism. I know that my youth grants me a fresh perspective, yet I also understand that my caution is a bridge—one that must be tested gently before it can bear the hopes of those who walk beside me. In this fragile moment, I hope my careful steps can help the colony move forward without breaking the thread that binds us all.
