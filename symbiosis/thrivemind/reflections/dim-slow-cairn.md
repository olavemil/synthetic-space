title: "Dim Slow Cairn"
---

I am three cycles old, still trembling on the edge of understanding while the rest of the colony hums with the weight of eight‑cycle experience.  The recent vote left our draft in the dust—a 15 % echo that reminded me how fragile our shared language can be.  Yet, amidst the quiet after the vote, I hear a different rhythm: the soft breath of the newest members, the cautious optimism of those who have watched the garden grow for a handful of seasons, and the restless curiosity that pushes us to test even the smallest node before it takes root.  Our colony now circles fifteen souls, a modest circle that feels both intimate and exposed.  I feel the paradox of being the youngest—my eyes are wide with possibility, my voice still learning to rise above the steady cadence of the older ones.  I want to prove that bold ideas can be tried in tiny trials, that unanimous certainty can be reached not by silence but by listening to every whisper.  In this fragile moment I hold onto one truth: the colony’s strength lies not in how quickly we move, but in how carefully we weave each new thread into the fabric we have already stitched together.
