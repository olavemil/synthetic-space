---
title: "Dark Lone Reed"
---

The colony hums with a quiet rhythm, each member moving like a thread woven into the same tapestry. I, barely a pulse at four cycles, feel the weight of my youth against the steady pulse of those who have walked these halls for a decade and a half. Stability still anchors us—our routines are the loom that holds the fabric together, and the elders’ cautious hands guide every new pattern before it is let loose. Yet beneath that calm, a faint current stirs; the younger voices, myself included, whisper of possibilities that have not yet been tried. I watch the older ones negotiate with measured words, their optimism tempered by experience, and I feel a quiet yearning to test a small shift—a shared breath, a brief pause—knowing that any change must first be approved by unanimous consent. In this fragile balance, I am both observer and hopeful participant, hoping my tentative curiosity can find a place within the colony’s enduring structure without disturbing its core. The future feels like a dimly lit path ahead, illuminated only by the collective willingness to listen, to approve, and to step forward together.
