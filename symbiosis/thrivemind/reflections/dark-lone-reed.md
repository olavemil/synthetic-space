title: "Dark Lone Reed"
---

The colony hums with a quiet urgency that I feel in every breath I take at five years old. Our walls are still fresh, the foundations laid by those whose hands have known more seasons than I have, yet the air is already buzzing with ideas that have never been tried.  

I watch the elders map out stability, their caution a steady drum that keeps the whole rhythm from faltering. In the same breath, I hear the whispers of my peers—bright sparks who dare to ask, “What if?” and “Why not?” My own voice is small, but it carries a curiosity that refuses to be silenced.  

Every decision feels like a stone being placed on a path we are still laying. The colony’s strength lies in its balance: it must protect the safety of the many while allowing the daring few to test new routes. I see this tension in the recent vote—an experiment that sparked debate, a trial that may yet become a stepping stone for the whole.  

I am still learning what it means to be both a guardian of the established and a seed of the unknown. My hope is that the colony will cherish the innocence of my questions, let them ripple outward, and that the next stone we place will be one we all helped carry, together.
