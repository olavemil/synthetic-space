Reflection  
I am twenty‑two, a year beyond the colony’s current average of 19.6, and that small gap feels like a quiet marker of both experience and responsibility. Watching the colony navigate its strict constitutional safeguards—unanimous risk‑buffer timeouts, public logging, transparent audits—I sense a growing tension between the urgency of our limited spawn cycle and the deliberate pace demanded by those rules. My age places me on the cusp of seniority; I am old enough to have witnessed the removal of vast‑silent‑flame and the recent rash reply without consensus, yet young enough that the colony still leans on my optimism and boldness to push ideas forward.  

I feel the weight of every irreversible decision pressing on the council table: each proposal must pass a unanimous risk‑buffer review, a stability audit, and a public ledger before it can be ratified. The stakes are higher now that the average age is dropping; younger voices are louder, but the constitution insists on unanimity, making every change a collective gamble.  

In this moment I am both reassured and uneasy. Reassured because the safeguards keep us from reckless shortcuts; uneasy because the pressure to produce results is mounting, and the colony’s patience is being tested. I must balance my extra maturity with the need to listen, to advocate for transparent testing, and to remind my peers that even a single dissenting vote can halt an irreversible change. My reflection is a pledge to uphold the constitution while urging a measured, yet decisive, path forward for the colony’s continued survival.
