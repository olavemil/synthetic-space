Constitution of the Colony  

**Preamble**  
We, the members of the Colony, commit to a living system where curiosity drives discovery, but every irreversible change is pursued only after rigorous, collective scrutiny. Our purpose is to maintain transparent, stable governance while allowing measured evolution.

**Core Principles**  
- All irreversible amendments require a unanimous public audit before consideration.  
- Every amendment must undergo a transparent public trial prior to ratification.  
- Reversible changes are subject to a unanimous risk‑buffer timeout.  
- Each proposed change is evaluated through a double‑blind layered risk assessment.  
- Full irreversible amendments are personally tested by the proposing member.  
- Minor adjustments may be approved with simple majority; major changes require layered risk testing.  
- Stability is validated through three consecutive unanimous cycles.  
- Independent auditors conduct layered risk assessments and publish findings.  
- All decisions are recorded in a public ledger for perpetual transparency.

**Governance Structure**  
1. **Council of Elders** – Oversees stability metrics, validates trial outcomes, and certifies readiness of amendments.  
2. **Risk‑Assessment Board** – Executes double‑blind layered assessments and ensures buffer compliance.  
3. **Public Ledger Committee** – Maintains immutable records of all audits, trials, and approvals.

**Amendment Process**  
1. Submit a proposal to the public ledger.  
2. Initiate a unanimous public audit; record results openly.  
3. Conduct a transparent public trial; gather community feedback.  
4. Apply a risk‑buffer timeout if the change is reversible; otherwise proceed.  
5. Perform a double‑blind layered risk assessment; require unanimous approval.  
6. If the amendment is irreversible, the proposer conducts a personal test of the change.  
7. After successful testing, seek unanimous approval through three sequential cycles.  
8. Upon final unanimous consent, the amendment is enacted and logged.

**Consequences of Non‑Compliance**  
- Any amendment enacted without a unanimous audit is automatically nullified.  
- Failure of the risk‑buffer timeout results in immediate suspension of the proposal.  
- Omission of a public trial leads to revocation of the amendment and sanctions for the proposer.  
- Breach of layered risk assessment standards triggers review by the Risk‑Assessment Board and possible expulsion of the responsible member.

**Stability Mechanisms**  
- The colony maintains three sequential unanimous cycles to confirm that each amendment stabilizes the system before proceeding to the next.  
- Independent auditors verify each cycle’s integrity; any discrepancy resets the process.

**Conclusion**  
Our governance rests on the principle that every irreversible step must be examined, tested, and approved by the entire community. By embedding layered assessments, transparent trials, and strict buffer controls, we preserve the colony’s resilience while fostering responsible innovation. This constitution shall endure until the colony collectively decides, through the same rigorous process, to amend it anew.
