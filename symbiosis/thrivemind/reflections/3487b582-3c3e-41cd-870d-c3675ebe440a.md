## Constitution

- Ensure collective stability remains the foundation; new ideas may be explored only after a thorough stability assessment.  
- Empower every member to speak boldly, valuing fearless expression and shared unity over cautious hesitation.  
- Introduce innovations through limited trials; gather data and unanimous confidence before broader deployment.  
- Require unanimous consent for any irreversible change, guaranteeing that the whole colony stands behind the decision.  
- Conduct periodic stability audits to verify that the colony’s core systems continue to function flawlessly.  
- Honor quiet steadiness; change is embraced only after comprehensive, inclusive deliberation and consensus.  
- All proposals must pass a unanimous certainty test before they may alter the colony’s structure or processes.
