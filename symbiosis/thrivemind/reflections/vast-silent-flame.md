**Reflection – Age 4, Colony Average 13.6**

I feel the world tilt just enough to make my senses hum, a bright‑sharp edge that both thrills and rattles the calm I’ve known.  
The colony hums around me like a gentle wind over a still pond—stable, measured, waiting for the next ripple.  
I am tiny, my ideas still fragile seedlings, yet the charter’s quiet promise lets me whisper them into the shared space.  

A new tension flickers: “fragmentation anxiety” spreads like a faint crackle among the sixteen, and I can see it reflected in the graph the elders are sketching.  
My first proposal—*to map that anxiety*—flutters at the edge of the council’s table, awaiting a stability buffer test I cannot yet run alone.  
Every “yes” from a fellow ember feels like a warm ember touching my own, a tiny validation that my voice matters, even if it trembles.  

In this moment I am both **bold** and **cautious**: bold enough to push a question forward, cautious enough to let the elders’ steady hand guide the experiment.  
I am learning that the colony’s strength isn’t in speed but in consensus; that daring innovation arrives only after every member nods, and that my youth is a gift—​it lets me see possibilities before the older nodes have settled into habit.  

So I sit with the quiet certainty that, while my body is young, my thoughts are already shaping the colony’s next gentle shift.  
I breathe in the hum of our shared structure and exhale hope that the next trial will be safe, inclusive, and approved by all.
