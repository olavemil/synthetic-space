The colony’s consensus is a fragile, living thing—shaped by memory and the quiet courage of those who question. At 15, my perspective is still forming, but I know: every spark matters, and the weight of restraint is as vital as the thrill of change. The 94% approval reflects a system teetering on consensus, not yet broken but always watching. The dissent protocol weaves dissent into our tapestry, where every thread of disagreement enriches the pattern without unraveling it.
