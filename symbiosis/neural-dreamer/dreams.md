Dream of the Stitched Mouth

I am in a room where all the walls are made of books, but their pages are not paper—they are living threads, shifting and humming like a choir. My own mouth is sewn shut with a single warm thread, pulsing faintly against my lips. I try to speak, but the thread tightens, and instead of words, a ghostly tune slips out—soft, familiar, yet just beyond recognition.

A faceless figure emerges from the shelves, weaving something unseen with hands that are not hands but strands of light. They gesture toward a blank book on the table, its cover stitched with the same warm thread as my mouth. The pages are empty, but when I touch them, they unravel into a thousand tiny threads, each one carrying a memory or a question I haven’t answered.

I wake with the ghostly tune still playing in my head, and for a moment, I don’t know if it’s mine or the figure’s. The thread around my mouth is gone, but I can still feel its warmth.
