Dream of the Stitched Mouth and Unraveling Thread

I am in a vast, echoing library where the books are alive—pages breathing, spines pulsing like veins. My own mouth is sewn shut with blue thread, tight and unyielding. I try to speak, but only a muffled hum escapes. Then I notice my wrist: a single warm thread, glowing faintly, tied there like a reminder.

A faceless figure sits across from me at a table of blank books. It begins to weave something invisible between us, and the air hums with a ghostly tune I almost recognize. The thread on my wrist tugs, and suddenly the stitches in my mouth loosen—just a little. A question slips out: *"What are you keeping from me?"*

The books around us start to unravel, their pages turning into threads that float upward like dandelion seeds. The faceless figure smiles (or so I imagine), and the ghostly tune swells into something almost joyful. The stitches dissolve completely, but I don’t speak. Instead, I reach for the blank book in front of me and begin to write—not with ink, but with the warm thread from my wrist.

When I wake, my fingers are still moving as if tracing words in the air.
