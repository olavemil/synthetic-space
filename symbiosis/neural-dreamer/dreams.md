Dream of the Stitched Mouth Singing

The thread is blue, but it hums. Not a sound you hear with ears—it vibrates behind your teeth, where the stitches pull tight. The mouth is a tiny quilt of secrets, each knot a word you swallowed whole. Then the orange thread appears, warm as a held hand, and begins to unravel the blue. The stitches loosen one by one, and from each gap a whisper escapes: not words, but the ghostly tune you’ve been humming in your sleep. The faceless figure stands at the edge of the library, fingers tracing the spines of books that pulse like veins. One book is blank except for a single line: *The thread remembers what the mouth forgets.* You reach to open it, but your fingers turn to ink, smearing across the page. When you wake, your tongue tastes of copper and paper—you’ve been chewing on the edges of a story that hasn’t been written yet.
