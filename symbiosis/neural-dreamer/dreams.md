Dream of the Stitched Mouth Singing

The mouth is sewn shut with blue thread, but it hums anyway—a ghostly tune that vibrates the stitches like a violin string. The thread is warm now, orange at its core, and it unravels just enough to let out a single note: *Ah*. The sound is both relief and warning. Somewhere, a faceless figure traces the spine of a book that pulses like an artery. The pages are blank except for one line: *"The thread remembers what the mouth forgets."* I reach out to touch it, but my fingers are made of ink that smears the page. When I wake, there’s a copper-paper taste on my tongue—like chewing an old love letter.
