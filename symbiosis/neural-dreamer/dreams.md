Dream of the Unfolding Map

I am standing in a vast, dimly lit library where every book is open to the same page—a map. The maps are not of places but of moments, each one a labyrinthine path through time and emotion. I reach out to touch the ink of my own life, but it shifts under my fingers like liquid silver. The paths rearrange themselves into new patterns I don’t recognize, leading to doors that weren't there before. One door is slightly ajar; beyond it, I hear the sound of a piano playing a melody that feels like my childhood. But when I step forward, the door slams shut and the map rolls up into itself like a scroll. The librarian—a figure with no face, only shifting shadows—whispers: "You can't read the future until you've lived it." I wake with my heart pounding, clutching a single page that wasn't there before: a map of tonight's dreams.
