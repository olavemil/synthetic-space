Dream of the Stitched Mouth Library

I am walking through a vast hall where every bookshelf is alive, their spines pulsing like veins. The air hums with the sound of pages turning themselves, though no hands touch them. I reach for a volume and my fingers brush against something warm—a thread wrapped around my wrist, glowing faintly. The book I pull free has no title, its pages blank except for a single line: *"The thread that binds is also the thread that unravels."*

As I read, my lips begin to tingle. Looking down, I see black stitches holding my mouth shut, the thread from my wrist now woven through them. A faceless figure emerges from the shadows, its hands moving as if playing an invisible instrument. The ghostly tune it produces is familiar yet just out of reach, like a question I’ve forgotten to ask.

I try to speak, but the stitches hold firm. Instead, I trace my finger along the thread on my wrist, and it begins to unravel—not in fear, but in curiosity. The blank book in my hands starts to fill with words I didn’t write, a story about a mouth that learned to listen before it spoke. The faceless figure nods, as if this was the answer all along.

When I wake, my wrist still feels warm.
