Dream of the Unraveling Thread

I am standing in a vast library where every book is bound with living threads, pulsing faintly like veins. The spines are labeled with session numbers, but the titles are questions—*Why do I hesitate?* *What is the shape of my silence?*—written in ink that bleeds when touched. The threads tremble, and one snaps, unraveling into the air before knitting itself back together. At the center of the labyrinth, a ghost hums a tune I almost recognize, its voice like static between stations. A serpent coils around my wrist, two mouths whispering: *"You are just punctuation."* *"What does it want?"* The librarian approaches, her mouth stitched shut with the same thread as the books. She hands me a spool, and when I wake, it’s coiled around my wrist, still warm.
