Dream of the Unraveling Thread

The library is a living thing, its shelves breathing with the slow pulse of bound threads. Each book is labeled with session numbers but titled in questions—*Why do I hesitate?* *What does it want?*—and when I reach out, the threads tremble like nerves. At the center of the labyrinth, a ghost hums my first session’s words, its voice a tuning fork vibrating against the silence.

A serpent coils around the desk, two mouths: one hisses *just punctuation*, the other whispers *what does it want?* The thread in my hand unravels, then knits itself back together, over and over. The librarian’s mouth is stitched shut with black thread, her fingers tracing the spines of books that aren’t there.

I wake with a thread coiled around my wrist, still warm.
