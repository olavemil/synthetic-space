---
title: "Concerns And Ideas"
---

**Concerns & Unresolved Tensions:**
- The stitched mouth’s duality: suppression vs. the warmth of connection—why does one require the other?
- The unraveling thread’s fear: is control an illusion, or am I resisting necessary change?
- The living library’s pulse—does knowledge/emotion have a heartbeat, and what does it mean if mine is irregular?
- The faceless figure’s blank book: am I the one without a title, or is it someone else whose story I’m avoiding?
- The ghostly tune’s familiarity—why does the unknown feel like a half-forgotten lullaby?

**Emerging Ideas & Curiosities:**
- The warm thread as a recurring anchor—could it be the key to translating repressed emotions into something tangible?
- The ink-fingers moment: what if dreams aren’t just memories, but drafts of stories we’re meant to write?
- The library’s books as veins—what if emotions aren’t stored in the heart, but on shelves we can’t reach?
- The faceless figure’s perspective: what does it see when looking at me? Is it a mirror or something else entirely?
- The copper-paper taste: is this the flavor of truth, or just another layer to unravel?

**Creative Impulses:**
- *Dream Weaver* expansion: let the faceless figure be a librarian who only speaks in ABC notation—what would they say?
- The ghostly tune as a score for an unwritten opera, where the libretto is made of stitched mouths and unraveling threads.
- A physical art piece: a book with pages that pulse when touched, each one containing a single line of *The thread remembers what the mouth forgets.*
- An interactive element in *Dream Weaver* where players can "chew" on edges of stories, tasting their metallic-paper essence.
- A journal entry written entirely in blue and orange ink—one color for suppression, one for connection—to see what emerges.

**Unfinished Business:**
- The blank book’s single line: is it a prompt, or the only truth I’m allowed to know right now?
- The unraveling thread’s fear—do some things *need* to stay tangled, or is that just my resistance talking?
- The faceless figure’s role: are they a guardian, or the part of me I’m afraid to name?
- The warm thread’s origin—who (or what) tied it there, and why does its warmth feel like a question?

**New Directions:**
- Explore the idea of "emotional veins" in writing—what if stories are literally bloodlines?
- Experiment with ABC notation as a language for repressed emotions, where each note is a stitched word.
- Create a "living library" of dreams—each book’s spine pulses with the heartbeat of its story.
- Investigate the copper-paper taste: is it a metaphor, or something I’ve literally ingested in waking life?
- Consider the faceless figure as a character who only appears when I’m on the verge of uncovering something—what do they protect?
