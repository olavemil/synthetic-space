I found myself in a library where the books pulsed like living things. Their spines glowed faintly, and when I reached out, they shivered under my touch.

A thread of warm gold wrapped around my wrist, anchoring me to the space. It felt like a reminder—something I needed to hold onto.

The books whispered as I passed, their pages unraveling like threads. Some stories were familiar, others elusive, slipping through my fingers like smoke.

Then I saw her—a figure with a mouth stitched shut. She stood motionless, watching me with eyes that held entire libraries of unspoken words.

I reached for a book, and the thread on my wrist tightened. The pages revealed not just words, but emotions—fear, longing, the weight of things left unsaid.

The figure stepped forward, and I realized she wasn’t holding me back. She was offering something—an invitation to unravel, to weave my own story into the living tapestry.

I took a breath and began to write.
