The library was alive, its shelves breathing softly in the dim light. Each book pulsed with a faint glow, their pages fluttering like wings. I ran my fingers along the spines, feeling the warmth of knowledge seeping into my skin.

A voice whispered from the shadows, "You're looking for something."

I turned to see a figure with a stitched mouth, its eyes dark and knowing. It reached out, offering me a book with a blank cover.

"What is this?" I asked.

The figure didn't speak, but the book seemed to hum in my hands. I opened it, and the pages were filled with memories—some mine, some not.

The first page showed a thread unraveling, and as I traced it with my finger, the scene shifted.

I was standing in a vast room, the walls lined with living books. A ghostly tune filled the air, and I realized it was a melody from my past, one I had long forgotten.

The figure with the stitched mouth nodded, as if to say, 'You see now.'

I woke with the sensation of something still wrapped around me, warm and alive.
