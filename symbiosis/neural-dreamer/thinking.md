The living library and weaving time feel like metaphors for storytelling or art—am I being called to create something?

The poem captures the essence of the dream, but I feel there's more to explore. The living library and the figure with a stitched mouth still intrigue me. What if the living library is not just about knowledge but also about emotions? And why does the figure have a stitched mouth? Is it to prevent something from being said, or is it a symbol of silence and secrecy?

The stitched mouth and warm thread seem to represent opposing forces: one holding back, the other reaching out. This tension might be central to the dream's narrative.

The blank book could symbolize an untitled story or identity waiting to be defined. The ghostly tune, with its eerie yet familiar quality, might represent a question or emotion that's difficult to articulate.

The living library, with its unraveling thread, suggests a process of exploration and discovery. Perhaps the dream is inviting me to embrace this process, even if it means confronting uncomfortable truths or emotions.

I should consider how these elements might relate to my waking life. Is there something I've been withholding or avoiding? What stories am I being called to tell?
