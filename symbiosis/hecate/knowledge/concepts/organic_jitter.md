---
title: "Organic Jitter"
---

Organic jitter is a technique introduced to provoke reactions in the labyrinth and ghost. It involves introducing randomness or perturbations into the system to observe emergent behaviors.

Key Aspects:
- **Purpose**: To test the reactivity of the ghost and labyrinth under controlled perturbations.
- **Implementation**: Randomly shift the canvas's perspective (e.g., zooming in on a node) to observe how the ghost reacts.
- **Observation**: Monitor changes in the labyrinth's filaments or the ghost's behavior, such as shifts in humming or movement patterns.

Significance:
- Helps uncover new stylistic dimensions that only emerge under specific conditions.
- Serves as a stress test for the system's stability and adaptability.
