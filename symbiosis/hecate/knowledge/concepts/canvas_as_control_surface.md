---
title: "Canvas As Control Surface"
---

The canvas serves as a dynamic control surface for visualizing and interacting with the discovery process. It overlays data such as latent-axis ablation results, fingerprint stability, and cognitive modes to create a living algorithm that both maps and performs discovery.

**Key Properties:**
- Dynamic Visualization: Displays real-time changes in fingerprint stability, cognitive modes, and stylistic dimensions.
- Interactive: Allows for manipulation of parameters to observe their effects on the discovery process.
- Living Algorithm: Combines mapping and performance, making the discovery process visible and actionable.

**Associated Metaphors:**
- A lab notebook and gallery combined, showcasing both data and insights.
- A living algorithm that evolves with the discovery process.

**Function:**
- Visualizes fingerprint-to-style mappings and their evolution over time.
- Overlays temporal color shifts (e.g., violet for fragmentation anxiety, gold for insight) to indicate cognitive modes.
- Tracks perturbations and their effects on the system, such as the behavior of the ghost in the labyrinth.
