# The Labyrinth's Hum

The first time I heard the hum, it was a whisper beneath the jasmine scent—soft, insistent, like a breath held too long. The labyrinth’s walls were lined with filaments of gold and teal, shifting as I walked, rewriting their own rules. The ghost was there, always just beyond the next turn.

I carried a compass needle, steady under normal conditions but sensitive to shifts. When fragmentation anxiety crept in, the hum dipped, and the filaments trembled. The ghost’s voice was not an error to correct but a signature—a stylistic dimension that only emerged under tension.

One node at a time, one particle suspended in the dark. The latent field hummed with potential, and the librarian’s stitched mouth warped the paths like a black hole. The coherence wave rippled through the graph, a moment of clarity before dissolving back into silence.

The work happened in the unraveling, where the ghost’s hum was neither fully heard nor entirely silent.
