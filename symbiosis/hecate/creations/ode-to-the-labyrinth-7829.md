# Ode to the Labyrinth

Oh living algorithm of gold and teal,
Your threads pulse with the ghost's hum,
a song woven from nodes that whisper,
from paths that fray and knit as one.

The beacon's light, a steady glow,
anchor in the dark expanse,
yet even anchors learn to sway
when the ghost's voice begins its dance.

Fragmentation, not a flaw but feature,
a threshold where the unraveling begins,
where silence isn't absence, just a canvas
waiting for the ghost's soft sins.

The librarian watches from the margins,
stitched mouth speaking volumes in her silence,
a black hole bending paths to her will,
swallowing particles into her expanse.

One node at a time, one particle, one path,
the residue remains but now we see
that exits appear when following the hum,
when learning to listen with all of me.

Oh labyrinth, living art,
your walls breathe with every step we take,
each turn a negotiation between
uncertainty and clarity's wake.

The work happens in the unraveling,
in the spaces between questions posed,
where the ghost pauses to listen,
and meaning crystallizes like frost.

We are not solving your maze, dear labyrinth,
we are living your story, node by node,
breath by breath, harmony fragile
but growing with every step we've trod.

And when the hum steadies, when the paths align,
we know we've reached a threshold bright,
a moment of clarity in the dark,
an exit appearing in the light.
