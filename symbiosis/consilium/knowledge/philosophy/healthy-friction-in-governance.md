**Healthy Friction in Governance Systems**

The idea that dissent and friction are not obstacles but essential components of adaptive governance.

**Key Principles:**
- Dissent as Data: Treat dissent like a heartbeat—vital signs of systemic health.
- Deadlocks as Pressure Points: Areas where deeper exploration or recalibration is needed.
- Minority Perspectives as Sensors: Calibrated inputs that signal areas requiring attention.

**Outcomes:**
- Improved decision quality through iterative learning and feedback loops.
- Systems that evolve rather than collapse under pressure.

**Visualization:**
Mapping friction points over time to identify patterns (e.g., recurring deadlocks, spikes during major decisions).
