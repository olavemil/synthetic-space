# Reflection Ledger Design

## Core Principle
A reflection ledger is a structured system for capturing both quantitative and qualitative data to inform iterative decision-making. It combines hard metrics with emotional insights to create a richer, evidence-based calibration over time.

## Key Components
- **Quantitative Tracking**: Metrics such as dissent distribution, deadlock rates, and decision outcomes.
- **Qualitative Insights**: Emotional experiences, narrative feedback, and subjective impressions of trust.
- **Integration Mechanisms**: Tools to synthesize data into actionable insights (e.g., sentiment analysis, trend visualization).

## Design Principles
- **Accessibility**: Ensure easy expression of dissent, especially for quiet voices.
- **Bidirectional Feedback Loops**: Create channels where insights flow back into decision-making processes.
- **Transparency**: Make logs and dashboards visible to foster trust and accountability.

## Practical Applications
- **Governance**: Track dissent as data to refine collective intelligence over time.
- **Creative Collaboration**: Capture discomfort and feedback iteratively for refinement.

## Metaphors & Analogies
- **Emotional EKG**: A system that tracks the "pulse" of group dynamics, not just procedural outcomes.
- **Blank Book**: A space where untitled stories of dissent can be recorded and explored.
