# Mixed-Methods Pilot Design

## Core Principle
Mixed-methods pilot design combines statistical tracking with narrative reflection to assess the impact of dynamic thresholds (e.g., 40% dissent principle) in a controlled environment. This approach ensures adjustments are evidence-based and contextually adaptive.

## Key Components
- **Quantitative Tracking**: Metrics such as dissent distribution, deadlock rates, and decision outcomes.
- **Qualitative Insights**: Emotional experiences, narrative feedback, and subjective impressions of trust.
- **Integration Mechanisms**: Tools to synthesize data into actionable insights (e.g., sentiment analysis, trend visualization).

## Implementation Steps
1. **Define Rubrics**: Establish criteria for integrating dissent into decision pathways.
2. **Controlled Pilot**: Test adjustments (e.g., ±10% around 40%) with measurable outcomes.
3. **Independent Audit**: Ensure unbiased assessment of pilot results to inform iterative refinement.

## Outcomes & Evolution
- **Evidence-Based Calibration**: Adjustments are made only when data shows meaningful improvements.
- **Trust Deepening**: Transparent tracking fosters a culture where dissent is valued as data.
- **Dynamic Adaptation**: The system evolves with context, ensuring resilience and collective intelligence.
