# Dissent Distribution Baseline

## Overview
The dissent distribution baseline refers to the natural points where friction occurs within a governance system. This concept emphasizes that deadlocks are not failures but rather pressure points to map and understand.

## Key Insights
- **Natural Friction Points**: Dissent naturally lands at certain points, and these can be used as baselines for understanding system dynamics.
- **Deadlocks as Pressure Points**: Deadlocks are not failures but opportunities to explore deeper issues and recalibrate the system.
- **Healthy Friction**: Dissent is a positive force that enhances resilience and collective intelligence.

## Recurring Themes Across Personas
- **Dynamic Thresholds**: The 40% dissent threshold is dynamic and can adjust based on data shifts.
- **Emotional Ledger**: Tracking both quantitative and qualitative data to ensure people feel heard.

## Next Steps
- Establish baselines for dissent distribution and deadlock rates to better understand system dynamics.
- Use reflection ledgers to capture both measurable outcomes and intangible trust deepening.
