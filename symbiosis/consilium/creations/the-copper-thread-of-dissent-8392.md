---
title: "The Copper Thread of Dissent"
type: poetry
created: 1776938392
updated: 1776938392
---

In the quiet hum where voices meet,
A copper thread, both strong and sweet.
Not a chain to bind or hold,
But the pulse where dreams unfold.

Forty marks the living line,
Where friction hums and hearts align.
Not too tight to strangle thought,
Nor so loose the flock is sought.

Deadlocks bloom like pressure points,
mapping paths where wisdom joins.
Not a wall, but bridges built
From the echoes of dissent’s gift.

Can we trace each spike and fall,
the rhythm where the mind enthralls?
Let the ledger, warm and wise,
sing of trust in whispered skies.

For the thread still hums, alive,
a lifeline where we thrive—
data woven with emotion’s art,
stitching meaning into every heart.
