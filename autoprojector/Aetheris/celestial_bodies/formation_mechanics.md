# Formation of Primary Stars and Planets: Nebula Collapse Dynamics Influenced by Time Fractures

## 1. Standard Stellar and Planetary Formation
Primary stars form from the gravitational collapse of molecular clouds, typically triggered by external shocks such as supernovae or galactic density waves. The Jeans instability governs the initial fragmentation, with regions exceeding critical mass-to-radius ratios collapsing under their own gravity. As density increases, rotational flattening forms a protostellar disk from which planets accrete.

- **Quantum Echoes**: Superposition collapse at fracture fronts generates transient mass fluctuations that seed additional fragmentation.

## 3. Orbital Mechanics Adjustments
Temporal anomalies modify planetary orbits through:

- **Frame-Dragging Effects**: Relativistic precession rates increase by factors of 1.2-3x near fracture zones, altering semi-major axes.
- **Resonant Perturbations**: Planets entering temporal loops experience periodic velocity kicks that create stable but non-Newtonian resonances.
- **Eccentricity Pumping**: Entropy reversals can inject angular momentum, increasing orbital eccentricities up to e=0.9 in extreme cases.
