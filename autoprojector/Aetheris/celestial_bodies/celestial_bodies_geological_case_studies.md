# Celestial Bodies – Geological Case Studies of Temporal Fractures

**Scope:** This document surveys concrete geological case studies that illustrate how *temporal fractures* — discrete, non‑linear ruptures in a planet’s causal timeline — re‑shape atmospheric chemistry, surface morphology, and evolutionary pathways. Each study is limited to a single planetary system and avoids overlap with the generic loop‑fracture discussion in `/timeline.md` or the atmospheric‑phase‑transition overview in `/celestial_bodies/celestial_bodies_case_studies/celestial_bodies_case_studies_earth_oxygenation/temporal_fractures_and_atmospheric_shifts.md`.

---

## 1. Conceptual Overview

| Element | Definition | Key Signature |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| **Temporal Fracture** | A localized rupture in the chronology of a planetary system that permits *non‑commutative* causal ordering (i.e., later events can retroactively influence earlier states). | Abrupt, irreversible changes in isotopic ratios, stratigraphic anomalies, or orbital phase shifts that cannot be explained by monotonic evolution. |
| **Fracture‑Induced Branch** | A divergent evolutionary branch that emerges only when a fracture re‑orders a critical reaction or sedimentation event. | Presence of “ghost” mineral phases, anomalous stratigraphic layers, or unexpected isotopic spikes. |
| **Feedback Loop** | The interaction between a fracture and the planetary feedback mechanisms (e.g., climate‑albedo, mantle convection) that can amplify or dampen the branch. | Repeated detection of the same anomaly across multiple epochs, suggesting a resonant feedback. |
