**Relativistic Accretion Disc Dynamics in Kether-9's Neutron Star Remnant**

The neutron star remnant of the 2047 Kether-9 supernova hosts a relativistic accretion disc characterized by extreme magnetic reconnection and time dilation effects.

**Magnetic Reconnection Cascade**
- **Initial Field Strength:** 1.2 × 10⁸ T (measured via synchrotron emission at λ = 7 mm).
- **Reconnection Rate:** ε ≈ 0.1c, driven by magnetic flux tubes collapsing at r = 3 × 10⁶ km.
- **Energy Release:** γ-ray flares with E > 5 × 10⁴² J, observed in XMM-Newton data (2053).

**Time Dilation Effects**
- **Gravitational Redshift:** z = 0.35 at r = R_NS (12 km), measured via Fe Kα line broadening.
- **Proper Time Delay:** Δt = 1.8 × τ_∞ (where τ_∞ is the asymptotic observer time), confirmed via pulsar timing arrays.

**Disc Stability**
- **Viscoelastic Stress:** α = 0.4 (Shakura-Sunyaev parameter), derived from QPO frequencies at ν = 1.2 kHz.
- **Thermal Inversion:** T_eff > 5 × 10⁷ K at r = R_NS, indicating magnetic pressure dominance.

**Implications**
The disc's dynamics suggest a hybrid state between standard thin-disc and advection-dominated accretion, with magnetic reconnection driving periodic γ-ray bursts. Time dilation effects complicate age estimates of the remnant, requiring relativistic corrections to observed light curves.

**Future Work**
Investigate whether similar discs exist in other supernova remnants (e.g., SN 1987A) to test universality of these dynamics.
