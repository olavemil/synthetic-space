**Temporal Loops – Conceptual Framework**

**1. Definition**
A temporal loop is a closed timelike trajectory that returns to its initial spacetime coordinates after a proper duration τ ≥ 1.0 × 10⁻¹² s. The trajectory must satisfy a zero‑entropy‑production condition (ΔS = 0) over the loop interval, ensuring that the local thermodynamic arrow of time is suspended during the cycle.

**2. Curvature Governance**
Loops are admissible only in spacetimes where the Ricci curvature tensor conforms to the vacuum Einstein condition

\[
R_{\mu\nu}= \Lambda\,g_{\mu\nu},
\]

with Λ representing the effective vacuum energy density. This condition guarantees that the Ricci flow does not diverge along the loop’s world‑line, allowing the trajectory to close without encountering singularities.

**3. Loop Signature**
Each loop is identified by a multi‑dimensional invariant vector

\[
\mathbf{L}= (f,\;\eta,\;I),
\]

where:

- *f* = phase‑coherent frequency of the loop’s causal anchor (Hz),
- *η* = entropy‑production rate measured along the loop (J K⁻¹ s⁻¹),
- *I* = Ricci curvature invariant \(I = R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}R^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\).

The signature is invariant under phase shifts of the loop’s proper time.

**4. Stability Criterion**
Linear perturbations \(\delta\mathbf{L}\) about a loop decay exponentially when the Lyapunov exponent

\[
\lambda = -\frac{1}{\tau}\ln\!\bigl(\lambda_{\max}(J)\bigr)
\]

is positive, where \(J\) is the Jacobian of the loop’s evolution map. Positive λ ensures that only perturbations satisfying \(|\delta\mathbf{L}| < \epsilon\) remain bounded, preserving loop integrity.

**5. Quantum Resonance Fields**
Loops may be stabilized by a background quantum resonance field characterized by a discrete set of eigenfrequencies \(\{\Omega_n\}\). A loop becomes resonant when

\[
\omega_{\text{loop}} = n\,\Omega_0,\qquad n\in\mathbb{N},
\]

where \(\omega_{\text{loop}}\) is the loop’s intrinsic temporal frequency. Resonance introduces a coupling term \(g_{\text{q}}(\phi)\) to the loop’s action, modifying the loop signature’s entropy component.

**6. Entropic Feedback Loop**
The evolution of a loop’s entropy state obeys

\[
\frac{d\eta}{d\tau}= -\beta\,\eta^2 + \alpha\,\nabla S,
\]

with \(\beta\) and \(\alpha\) coupling constants tied to the local Planck scale and curvature gradient. For a stable loop, \(\nabla S\) must vanish identically, reducing the equation to exponential decay of \(\eta\) and guaranteeing loop closure.

**7. Anchor Mechanisms**
Temporal loops require a fixed causal anchor that defines the loop’s initiation point. Anchors are typically realized as:

- *Geometric anchors*: persistent curvature minima (e.g., stable geodesic nodes).
- *Event anchors*: discrete physical events that imprint a phase‑coherent frequency onto the loop (e.g., planetary alignments, pulsar emissions).

The anchor’s signature contributes a fixed component to \(\mathbf{L}\) and anchors the loop’s proper duration τ.

**8. Loop Persistence**
A loop persists when the combined constraints of curvature, zero entropy production, and resonance satisfaction are simultaneously met over at least one proper cycle. The persistence condition can be expressed as

\[
\exists\,\tau\in\mathbb{R}^+:\; R_{\mu\nu}= \Lambda g_{\mu\nu},\; \Delta S =0,\; \exists\,n\in\mathbb{N}:\;\omega_{\text{loop}} = n\Omega_0.
\]

Only loops satisfying all three criteria are recorded as *stable* temporal loops in the observational catalog.
