### Alignment of Dust Grains Due to Magnetic Field Amplification During Kether-9 Supernova Shockwave Interaction

During the interaction of the Kether-9 supernova shockwave with NGC 6946-23, magnetic field amplification occurred through the compression of pre-existing interstellar fields. This process induced alignment in dust grains within the molecular cloud, observable through polarized emission at submillimeter wavelengths.

**Mechanism:**
1. **Field Amplification:** The shockwave compressed initial fields (B₀ = 3.2 × 10⁻¹² T) to B₁ ≈ 2.8 × 10⁻¹ T, injecting ~1.7 × 10⁴² J into the magnetic topology.
2. **Dust Grain Alignment:** The amplified field induced torques on paramagnetic dust grains (radius ~0.1 µm), aligning their long axes perpendicular to the field direction.
3. **Polarization Signature:** The alignment produced linear polarization of ~10% at λ = 850 µm, with position angle θ aligned to the local field orientation.

**Observational Evidence:**
- Polarized emission maps revealed coherent alignment over ~0.3 pc scales, consistent with theoretical models of shock-induced magnetic compression.
- The alignment persisted for ~1.2 × 10³ years post-shock, as inferred from dust temperature gradients (T_dust ≈ 25 K).

**Implications:**
The alignment process may have seeded large-scale magnetic structures in the post-shock medium, influencing subsequent star formation efficiency. Further analysis of polarization spectra could constrain dust composition and grain size distributions in the remnant.
