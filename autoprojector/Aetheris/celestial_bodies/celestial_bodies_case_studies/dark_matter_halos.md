# Investigation of Dark Matter Halos Around Exoplanetary Systems

## Detection Methods
Dark matter halos around exoplanets are inferred through indirect gravitational effects. Key detection methods include:

1. **Gravitational Microlensing**
   - Exoplanets act as lenses, magnifying background stars.
   - Dark matter halos introduce additional lensing anomalies.

2. **Orbital Perturbations**
   - Unexpected deviations in exoplanet orbits suggest unseen mass.
   - High-precision radial velocity measurements detect these shifts.

3. **Gravitational Lensing of Light**
   - Dark matter halos bend light from distant galaxies.
   - Observed as distorted or multiple images of background sources.

## Effects on Planetary Orbits
Dark matter halos influence exoplanet dynamics through:

- **Precession of Orbital Elements**
  - Halos cause long-term changes in orbital inclination and eccentricity.
- **Stability of Resonances**
  - Dark matter can disrupt or stabilize orbital resonances.
- **Ejection of Planets**
  - High-mass halos may eject planets from their systems.

## Anomalies: Unexpected Gravitational Lensing Events
Observed lensing events that deviate from standard models suggest:

- **Temporal Fractures**
  - Sudden, brief reversals in lensing effects.
- **Spacetime Distortions**
  - Non-linear bending of light paths.

## Speculative Links to Temporal Fractures
Hypotheses propose dark matter halos may:

- **Stabilize Temporal Loops**
  - Halos could anchor closed timelike curves.
- **Amplify Spacetime Distortions**
  - Dark matter density fluctuations may trigger fractures.

Further study requires high-resolution gravitational wave detectors and next-generation telescopes.
