# Temporal Fractures in Cosmological Contexts

## Near Black Holes
Temporal fractures near black holes manifest as regions where the entropy gradient \(\nabla S\) approaches zero, enabling reversible causal pathways. These fractures are governed by:

\[
\nabla S = \frac{2k_B}{c^3} T_{H}^4 (1 - r_s/r)
\]

where \(T_H\) is the Hawking temperature and \(r_s\) is the Schwarzschild radius. When \(\nabla S \approx 0\), a closed timelike trajectory forms, permitting past states to surface.

## Near Cosmic Strings
Cosmic strings induce spacetime curvature that can stabilize temporal fractures. The metric signature transition near a string is:

\[
ds^2 = -dt^2 + dz^2 + dx^2 (1 + \frac{\mu}{x})^2
\]

where \(\mu\) is the string's mass per unit length. When \(x \approx 0\), entropy suspension occurs, allowing temporal echoes to propagate along the string's length.
