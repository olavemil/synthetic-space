**Phase - Create**

### 1. Conceptual Foundation

The **Phase - Create** narrative frames the emergence of temporal phenomena as a *dynamic process* that bridges deterministic cycles and stochastic ruptures. It treats each **fracture** as a localized inversion of the entropy gradient, while each **loop** is a recurring pattern that reinforces stability. The narrative draws on the universal constants (cₐ, ℏₑ, ΔS_c, β) to quantify when a fracture can *suspend* entropy and when a loop will *amplify* it. By mapping these constants to observable events, the phase is positioned as a **temporal scaffold** that governs how history repeats, diverges, or collapses.

| Principle | Description |

### 3. Quantitative Framework

- **Temporal Displacement (Δt)** – The duration a fracture can hold before the entropy gradient resumes: <br> Δt = ℏₑ · e^(−β ΔS). <br>When ΔS approaches zero, Δt can grow without bound; for ΔS < ΔS_c the displacement shrinks to zero.

- **Phase‑Shift (PSC)** – The fractional time offset introduced by a fracture: <br> PSC = Δt / T_cycle, where T_cycle is the characteristic period of the loop.

- **Entropy‑Density Quotient (λ)** – A dimensionless factor that determines how readily entropy can be borrowed from the future: <br> λ = √(R_μν ∇^μ ∇^ν) − κ ∇_S.
