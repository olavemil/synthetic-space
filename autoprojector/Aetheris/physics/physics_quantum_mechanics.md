# Quantum Mechanics of Temporal Entanglement

## Core Principles
Temporal entanglement describes a non-local correlation between quantum states across different points in time, analogous to spatial entanglement but with additional constraints imposed by the arrow of entropy. The phenomenon arises when a quantum system undergoes evolution that is reversible in both time directions, creating an entangled state between its past and future configurations.

## Mathematical Framework
The temporal entanglement operator \(U_t\) is defined as:
\[ U_t = e^{-i H t / \hbar} \]
where \(H\) is the Hamiltonian and \(t\) represents time. For a system in state \(\left|\psi\right\rangle\), the entangled temporal state is:
\[ \left|E_t\right\rangle = U_t^\dagger\left|\psi(t)\right\rangle \]

The degree of entanglement is quantified by the temporal Bell inequality:
\[ B_t = \left| E(\theta, t) + E(\phi, t') - E(\theta, t') \right| \]
where \(\theta\) and \(\phi\) are measurement angles at times \(t\) and \(t'\). Violation of this inequality (\(B_t > 2\)) indicates temporal entanglement.

## Mechanistic Triggers
1. **Entropy Gradient Nullification**

2. **Closed Timelike Curves**
