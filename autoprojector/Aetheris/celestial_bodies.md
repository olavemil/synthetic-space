## Temporal Influences on Celestial Bodies

### Stellar Evolution and Timescales
Primary stars dominate the observable universe, yet their life cycles are intimately tied to temporal behavior of surrounding space.

- **Main Sequence Burning**: Hydrogen fusion dominates; luminosity scales with mass.
  - O-type stars: 2–10 Myr
  - G-type (Sun-like): ~1 Gyr
- **Red Giant Phase**: Core contracts, outer envelope expands; planets may be engulfed.
  - Massive stars: <10 Myr to red giant
- **End States**: Supernovae (O/B types) or white dwarfs (G/K/M).

### Planetary Formation Dynamics
- **Accretion Rates**: 10⁻⁶ M☉/yr for a protostar.
- **Orbital Migration**: Giant planets migrate due to disk torques (Type I/II migration).
- **Resonance Locking**: Orbital resonances stabilize planetary systems (e.g., Jupiter-Saturn 5:2).

### Orbital Dynamics and Stability
- **Gravitational Interactions**: Shape eccentricity variations (e.g., Mars' 150,000-year cycle).
- **Long-term Stability**: Lyapunov timescales for chaotic systems (e.g., Uranus-Neptune ~10⁸ yr).

- **Examples**:
