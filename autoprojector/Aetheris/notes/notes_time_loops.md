Potential Time Loops
Definition: A potential time loop is a hypothesized temporal configuration wherein a sequence of events repeats within a bounded interval, exhibiting repeatable causal patterns while permitting limited variation in outcome.

Key attributes:
- Temporal interval: Must be defined by a repeatable cycle, e.g., the 10 532‑year Mayan Long Count, the 1 803‑year Saros eclipse cycle, or the 27‑year agricultural commodity price cycle.
- Trigger mechanism: Must involve a deterministic event that can be mathematically modeled, such as a planetary alignment or a market price threshold.
- Variation allowance: Permits up to 5 % deviation in non‑essential variables without breaking loop integrity.

Verification protocol:
- Identify at least three historically documented cycles that satisfy the interval and trigger criteria.
- Demonstrate a statistical correlation (Pearson r ≥ 0.7) between trigger event and subsequent outcome across cycles.
- Ensure that variations remain within the defined tolerance without altering the loop’s structural pattern.

Categories of potential loops:
1. Astronomical – cycles driven by celestial mechanics, e.g., Saros eclipse cycles influencing naval dominance patterns.
2. Economic – periodic market fluctuations tied to environmental cycles, e.g., wheat price spikes every 27 years correlated with solar activity.
3. Cultural – recurring sociopolitical uprisings every ~33 years linked to generational cohort turnover.
Each category must satisfy the verification protocol to be classified as a confirmed potential loop.
