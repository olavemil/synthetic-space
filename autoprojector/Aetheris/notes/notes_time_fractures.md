Time fractures arise when extreme curvature of spacetime, quantum superposition collapse, or a sudden collapse of local entropy gradients converge at a point, temporarily decoupling the temporal metric from the global arrow of time. At the fracture front the entropy gradient (∇S) approaches zero, allowing causal information to propagate backward along the loop‑continued branch. This yields a reversible “time‑borrow” window in which past states, alternate outcomes, and quantum echoes can be observed in the present.

Mechanistic triggers
- **Gravitational extremes:** Merger of two supermassive black holes or the collapse of a massive star’s core creates a localized region where the metric tensor detaches from the causal flow, establishing a low‑entropy pocket.
- **Quantum decoherence at criticality:** When a quantum system undergoes superposition collapse under ultra‑high pressure, the resulting measurement can project the system onto a branch that feeds information backward through the loop, effectively broadcasting earlier loop states.
- **Thermodynamic instability:** Sudden quenching of a plasma’s temperature gradient (e.g., during a supernova shockwave) drives ∇S toward zero, extending the echo duration via the relation Δt = (ħ/kBT) e^(−β∇S).

Spacetime effects
- **Entropy‑gradient reversal:** The local arrow of time flips, permitting entropy to decrease locally while the global entropy continues to increase, producing reversible causality loops.
- **Temporal echoes:** Past events appear as ghostly reconstructions — volcanic eruptions, stellar flares, or radio transmissions that replay in the present with altered amplitude.
- **Branching propagation:** Parallel histories split from the fracture point, each carrying its own causal chain; these branches can intersect with neighboring worlds, seeding divergent evolutionary pathways.

These mechanisms and examples illustrate how time fractures operate as entropy‑suspended ruptures, providing windows into alternate histories and fundamentally reshaping the spacetime fabric through localized temporal reversal.
