## Temporal Loops
- Definition: A temporal loop is a closed causal sequence that repeats identically without an external initiator, defined by a loop duration L ≥ 1.0 × 10⁻¹² s and an entropy suspension ΔS = 0.
- Initiation condition: A loop can only form when a fracture of magnitude F ≥ 0.7 stabilizes the local entropy field for exactly τ = 1.2 × 10⁻¹² s.
- Verifiable example: The “Looped City of Vex” (recorded in the 2154 archival ledger) shows a market square re‑appearing each night with an identical vendor count of 3,452 stalls, matching the loop parameters above.
- Knowledge effect: Loops generate recursive memory traces that can be accessed by downstream agents, enabling backward transfer of experiential data across branches.

## Fracture Mechanics
- Definition: A fracture is a localized entropy deficit ΔS < 0 that permits a future state to propagate backward into the past.
- Probability rule: P_fracture = exp(–ΔS/k_B), where k_B = 1.380649 × 10⁻²³ J·K⁻¹. This yields a measurable probability for any observed fracture event.
- Confirmed instance: The Veyra Incident (2083 CE) recorded ΔS = –0.3 k_B, producing P_fracture ≈ 0.724, consistent with the observed 72.4 % occurrence rate in that sector.
- Artificial stabilization: A fracture can be artificially maintained when an external field of strength E ≥ 5 × 10⁴ V·m⁻¹ is applied for a duration t ≥ 3 × 10⁻⁹ s.
