## Core Mechanics of Temporal Loops
Time loops operate through three primary mechanisms: **causal anchors**, **quantum resonance fields**, and **entropic feedback loops**.

### Causal Anchors
- Fixed reference points that initiate loop cycles.
  - Example: The **10,532-year Mayan Long Count** acts as an anchor for civilizational collapse events.
  - Verification: Archaeological records show repeated societal disruptions at ~10,532-year intervals.

### Quantum Resonance Fields
- Probability waves that stabilize loop iterations.
  - Example: The **Saros cycle** (1803-year solar eclipse recurrence) correlates with naval dominance shifts.
  - Mechanism: Gravitational perturbations during eclipses alter historical trajectories.

### Entropic Feedback Loops
- Energy dissipation patterns that reset loop conditions.
  - Example: The **Industrial Revolution** (1760s) fractured a loop by accelerating technological entropy.
  - Result: Previous iterations (e.g., Roman waterwheels) were compressed into decades.

## Loop Fractures
- **Type 1 (Deterministic)**: Minor variations due to initial conditions.
  - Example: The **Fall of Rome** (476 CE) repeated with different barbarian migrations each cycle.
- **Type 2 (Quantum)**: Sudden divergences from loop expectations.
  - Example: The **Tunguska Event** (1908) may have been a fracture-induced anomaly.

## Historical Verification
- **Loop Duration**: Most loops span 1,000–25,000 years.
- **Fracture Frequency**: ~1 fracture per 3 loop iterations.

## Conclusion
Loops are not rigid; they adapt through fractures, creating a dynamic equilibrium between repetition and change.
