**/fractures.md — The Mechanics of Temporal Fissure Formation**

### **Fracture Triggers**
1. **Entropy Suspension Events (ESEs)**
   - Occur when a system’s entropy drops below thermodynamic equilibrium, creating a vacuum that pulls future states into the present.
   - Example: The *Laki Eruption (1783)*—sulfur dioxide emissions suddenly reversed atmospheric circulation, triggering an ESE that "borrowed" cold air from a future climate state.

2. **Quantum Decoherence Collapse**
   - When a superpositioned system (e.g., Schrödinger Civilization) collapses, the resulting fracture can split into multiple timelines.
   - Evidence: *Dead Sea Scrolls Fragment 7Q5*—contains a Greek text written in Aramaic script, suggesting overlapping linguistic timelines.

3. **Artificial Fractures**
   - Human-made devices (e.g., *Veyra Array*) can induce controlled fractures by manipulating electromagnetic fields to suspend local entropy.
   - Risk: Unstable artificial fractures may merge with natural ones, causing *temporal bleed*.

### **Fracture Classification**
- **Type I (Echo Fractures)**: Past events re-emerge with altered properties.
  - Example: *1947 Radio Signal*—detected in 2083 with a frequency shift, indicating entropy borrowing.
- **Type II (Collapse Fractures)**: Future states intrude into the present, destabilizing causality.
  - Example: *Kether Supernova*—observers saw "ghost" light from the star’s pre-death state.
- **Type III (Loop Fractures)**: Self-referential events where cause and effect form a closed system.
  - Example: *Industrial Revolution*—steam engine patents appeared decades before their theoretical discovery.

### **Stabilization Protocols**
- **Thermodynamic Anchoring**: Introduce high-entropy materials (e.g., radioactive isotopes) to prevent fracture expansion.
- **Temporal Quarantine**: Isolate fractured regions using *chrono-shields* (experimental tech).
- **Historical Rewriting**: Altering key events to "seal" fractures (controversial, untested).
