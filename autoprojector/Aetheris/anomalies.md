### 1. **Gravitational Singularities**
**Observation:** Spacetime curvature becomes infinite, typically inside black holes.
- **Effect on Environment:** Light bends around them (gravitational lensing), and time runs slower near the event horizon.
- **Effect on Society:** Navigation fails; long-term exposure may disrupt circadian rhythms in space missions.
- **Mathematical Formulation:**
  - Schwarzschild radius: \( R_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \)
  - Time dilation factor: \( t' = t \sqrt{1 - \frac{R_s}{r}} \)

### 2. **Quantum Vacuum Fluctuations**
**Observation:** Spontaneous particle–antiparticle creation and annihilation in empty space.
- **Effect on Environment:** Casimir effect between plates; Hawking radiation from black holes.
- **Effect on Society:** Potential for zero-point energy extraction; decoherence in quantum computers.
- **Mathematical Formulation:**
  - Vacuum energy density: \( \rho_{vac} = -\frac{\pi^2}{720} \left(\frac{m_e c}{\hbar}\right)^4 T_K^4 \)
  - Hawking temperature: \( T_H = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi G M k_B} \)

**Note:** The Casimir effect was first measured in 1997 with nanometer precision.

### 3. **Dark Matter Halos**
**Observation:** Unseen mass surrounding galaxies, inferred from rotation curves.
- **Effect on Environment:** Alters galactic dynamics and may stabilize interstellar gas clouds.
- **Effect on Society:** Influences deep-space navigation via lensing.
- **Mathematical Formulation:**
  - Density profile: \( \rho(r) = \frac{\rho_0}{1 + (r/r_c)^2} \)
