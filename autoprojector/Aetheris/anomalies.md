### 1. **Gravitational Singularities**
**Observation:** Extreme spacetime curvature near compact objects.
- **Effect on Environment:**
  - Light deflection (lensing) creates Einstein rings and cross-like images.
  - Time dilation causes clock synchronization errors in GPS systems near Earth's gravitational well (≈7 μs/day).
  - Tidal forces can spaghettify objects near neutron stars (e.g., PSR J1748-2021B).
  - **Example:** The Event Horizon Telescope observed M87* with a shadow diameter of ~42 μas, consistent within 10% of Schwarzschild radius predictions.
- **Effect on Society:**
  - Spacecraft navigation near Jupiter requires relativistic corrections to avoid orbit decay.
  - Long-duration missions (e.g., Mars colonization) require atomic clocks with stability <10⁻¹⁸ to account for gravitational redshift.
- **Mathematical Formulation:**
  - Schwarzschild radius: \( R_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \)
  - Time dilation factor: \( t' = t \sqrt{1 - \frac{R_s}{r}} \)
  - Tidal acceleration difference (across body of radius R): \( \Delta a = \frac{2GM}{r^3} R \)
