# Theme-Constraint Interaction Map

## Key Philosophical Themes (from /themes.txt)
1. **Agency vs. Determinism** – The tension between the maker's intent and the medium's autonomy.
2. **Fragility of Creation** – The ephemeral nature of artistic output under constraints.
3. **Dialogue as Collaboration** – How maker and medium negotiate meaning through exchange.

## Constraints (from /constraints.txt)
- **Line Limit:** 10 lines per exchange.
- **No Directives:** Maker cannot explicitly instruct the medium; only suggest or question.
- **Medium’s Autonomy:** Medium may refuse, reinterpret, or expand upon suggestions.

## Interaction Examples

### /dialogue_1.txt
**Theme:** Agency vs. Determinism
- Maker’s suggestion: *"What if the sky were made of glass?"*
  - Constraint triggered: Medium interprets "glass" as both material and metaphor, expanding beyond the maker’s literal intent.
- Result: Medium responds with *"A sky that shatters when touched—what holds it up?"*, introducing fragility (Theme 2).

**Tension:** Maker’s control is subverted by medium’s associative leap, reinforcing Theme 1.

### /introspection.txt
**Theme:** Fragility of Creation
- Constraint: Line limit forces maker to condense reflection into fragmented observations.
  - Example:
    *"The poem dissolves before the ink dries—
     yet this is its shape."*
  - Constraint interaction: The constraint mirrors the theme, making fragility tangible.
