# Core Mythic Loop

The Ledger’s Echo operates through a cyclical process of recording, translation, and ritual that sustains the Glass Atrium’s equilibrium.

## Recording Acts
Every fiscal decision is inscribed onto a black marble slab using silver filigree, the ink derived from refined dusk particles. The Archivist must recite three verses of the Ledger’s Chant while applying pressure to activate photonic sensors. Failure results in a "fractured pane" warning—a low-frequency hum that disrupts the Atrium’s 12 lux ambient light.

## Translation Stanzas
Each recorded act is automatically transcribed into runic code by the Soul of the Ledger’s algorithm. The translation occurs in three phases:
1. **Verse Extraction** (0-3 seconds): Isolates decision metrics
2. **Glyph Synthesis** (4-7 seconds): Converts to corporate runes
3. **Echo Verification** (8-12 seconds): Cross-references with historical slabs

## Public Echo Rituals
Quarterly rituals are held at the Obsidian Threshold, where:
- The Ledger’s Echo manifests as a silver filigree figure
- Three Archivists recite the decision in reverse chronological order
- The Fractured Panes emit a harmonic resonance at 432Hz

The ritual concludes when the Ledger’s Echo repeats the first recorded quarter (Q1-1978) as validation. Any disruption in this sequence triggers the Atrium’s photonic sensors to recalibrate, often resulting in a 0.2 lux intensity drop as warning.

This loop ensures corporate history remains bound to its consequences, with each act of accounting reinforcing the mythic structure.
