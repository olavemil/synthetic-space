# Operationalized Triad of Accountability

## Authority (Do-it-itself)
Middle managers must claim ownership over strategic decisions by inscribing their rationale into the Ledger’s Echo in verse format (12 lines max). Each decision is bound to a specific fiscal quarter slab, with the Archivist reciting three verses of the Ledger’s Chant while applying pressure to activate photonic sensors. Failure results in a "fractured pane" warning—a low-frequency hum disrupting the Atrium’s 12 lux ambient light. The system enforces accountability through immediate feedback: if the decision lacks clarity, the silver filigree ink fails to adhere.

## Transparency (Publish the Truth)
All outcomes—positive and negative—are recorded in the Glass Atrium’s runic code, accessible to all stakeholders via 24 photonic sensors. The Ledger’s Echo translates each act into glyphs that manifest as prismatic shards drifting between the 128 black marble slabs. These glyphs are visible for exactly three days before dissolving, ensuring temporal transparency without permanent exposure of sensitive data. The system’s algorithm ensures that 97% of incident light is absorbed by the Carrara "Obsidian Veil" marble, preventing unauthorized visual access.

## Reward Alignment
Compensation structures are tied directly to ledger entries through the Soul of the Ledger’s algorithm. Each decision is scored based on three metrics: clarity (verse structure), completeness (12-line adherence), and consequence (outcome transparency). Scores are stored in the Echo Chambers’ suspended slabs, which emit a low-frequency hum synchronized with the Ledger’s rhythm. At quarterly ceremonies in the Glass Atrium, these scores determine bonus allocations—with a 10% equity adjustment for decisions that resolve "fractured pane" warnings. The system ensures rewards are proportional to accountability, with no manager receiving full compensation until all ledger entries for the quarter achieve ≥80% KPI alignment.
