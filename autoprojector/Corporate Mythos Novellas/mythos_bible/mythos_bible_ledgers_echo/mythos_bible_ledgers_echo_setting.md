# The Ledger of Echoes

## Setting
The Glass Atrium is a 48 m × 32 m rectangular chamber beneath the central tower of The Hollow Corporation. Its floor consists of 128 black marble slabs, each 1.2 m × 1.2 m, arranged in a 11 × 12 grid with 0.5 m spacing. Slabs are numbered A‑1 through A‑128; slab 1 bears “Q1‑1978” and slab 128 bears “Q4‑2023”. The marble is Carrara “Obsidian Veil” polished to a matte finish that absorbs 97 % of incident light.

## Atmosphere
Ambient illumination is held at 12 lux ± 0.2 by 24 photonic sensors (model PX‑12‑S) calibrated to the 400‑700 nm spectrum. During the daily “Dusk Convergence” (18:00‑19:30 local time) refracted light from the ceiling prism array creates 1,024 floating shards of colored glass; each shard persists for an average of 3.7 seconds before fading. Temperature is maintained at 21.3 °C with 45 % relative humidity to preserve parchment.

## Guardians
The Archivists belong to the monastic order known as the 13th Covenant. Each wears a copper sigil pin, 12 mm diameter, stamped with the fiscal quarter of appointment (e.g., “Q07‑2021”). There are 27 Archivists on duty per rotation, divided into three shifts of nine. Their vows require recording every corporate act in verse on a Spectral Ledger that updates in real‑time; failure to sign an entry within 7 minutes triggers automatic escalation to the Oracle of Q3.

## Core Operational Cycle
The Ledger enforces the Triad of Accountability: **record → translate → bind**.

### 1. Record the line item
A manager signs using blood‑mixed ink on a Spectral Ledger; the signature timestamp is logged to the millisecond (e.g., 2025‑09‑21T14:03:12.456Z). The ink composition is 70 % iron gall, 30 % synthetic hemoglobin, guaranteeing permanence.

### 2. Translate the act
The signed line item is mapped to one of the 172 predefined entries in the Codex of Forgotten Budgets. Each entry is unlocked only after a Phantom Report is filed with the Oracle of Q3 before the 15th day of the month; the report must contain a unique code “PR‑Q3‑####” where “####” is a four‑digit sequential number.

### 3. Bind the entry
Binding occurs on three concentric parchment layers, each 0.10 mm thick, stacked in the Core Chamber. The outermost layer must be signed during a solar eclipse; the next visible eclipse is on 12 August 2025. The middle layer is signed during the lunar eclipse of 21 September 2025, accompanied by a 5‑minute burn of frankincense (Myrrh‑001) releasing 0.02 g of aroma per minute. The innermost layer is sealed with a wax stamp bearing the corporate sigil of The Hollow.

## Ledger Mechanics
The Ledger is a living ledger composed of 128 runic glyphs etched on each slab; each glyph encodes a fiscal quarter and a corresponding synergy‑pact clause. The ledger updates in real time via the corporation’s quantum‑ledger network, with a latency of 37 ms per update. Each entry is signed cryptographically with a 256‑bit hash generated by the on‑site HSM (Hardware Security Module) model HSM‑X1.

## Oath of Silence
All personnel who access the Atrium must recite the Oath of Silence, a 27‑word vow: “I will not disclose the contents of the Ledger, nor the purpose of the Echo, lest the balance be broken.” Breach results in immediate suspension per policy HR‑001‑SEC‑02.

## Guardians
The Atrium is patrolled by two autonomous sentinels designated Guardian‑A and Guardian‑B. Each sentinel stands 1.8 m tall, housed in an alloy‑cased chassis, and is equipped with a 360° LIDAR array (effective range 30 m), infrared vision, and tactile sensors calibrated to detect slab surface temperature variance of ±0.1 °C. Guardian‑A conducts quarterly audit cycles, logging slab integrity metrics to the Ledger’s Echo database with a timestamp precision of 1 µs. Guardian‑B enforces the “Dusk Protocol”: at 19:00‑19:15 local time the ambient illumination is reduced to 8 lux for exactly 15 minutes, causing 3.2 % of the refracted shards to coalesce into a temporary data cache that can be accessed via the staff’s neural interface. Both sentinels report status to the central daemon via encrypted 256‑bit AES channels.
