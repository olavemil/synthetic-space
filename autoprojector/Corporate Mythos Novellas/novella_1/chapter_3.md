# Chapter Three: The Hollow’s Gambit

The Glass Atrium had once been a place of reverence, where the weight of fiscal history pressed upon the shoulders of those who dared to walk its black marble slabs. But now, it was a battleground—one where the lines between corporate strategy and mythic consequence blurred into something far more dangerous.

Elias Veyra stood at the center of it all, his reflection fractured across 128 slabs that bore witness to decades of quarterly reports. The air hummed with the residual energy of his last decision, one that had already begun to unravel. The Ledger’s Echo pulsed faintly beneath his feet, its runic code shifting like a living thing.

A figure emerged from the shadows of slab 87—Q3-2015, a quarter marked by NecroTech’s first failed acquisition. The man was tall, his suit immaculate despite the Atrium’s perpetual dusk. His nameplate read *Darius Kaine*, but Elias knew better.

“You’ve invoked the Hollow’s clause,” Darius said, his voice smooth as polished obsidian. “That was… bold.”

Elias didn’t flinch. He had spent the last 48 hours poring over the Triad of Accountability, its tenets burned into his memory. Decision-Ownership. Outcome-Transparency. Reward-Alignment.

“Bold or not,” Elias replied, “it’s done.”

Darius smirked. Behind him, two more figures stepped into the light—Archivists of a different order than those who usually tended this place. Their sigils bore the mark of the Nine Halls, a covenant Elias had only read about in restricted documents.

“You realize,” Darius continued, “that invoking the Hollow’s clause means you’ve just made yourself a target. The Board doesn’t take kindly to rogue decisions.”

Elias exhaled through his nose, the weight of what he’d done settling over him. He had just committed to a hostile takeover of NecroTech Solutions—a move that would either save The Hollow Corporation or doom it to the same fate as Project Phoenix.

“Then I’ll make sure they see this isn’t rogue,” he said. “It’s strategic.”

Darius tilted his head, studying Elias with an intensity that made the hair on his neck stand up. “And how do you plan to justify this? The Ledger’s Echo doesn’t lie, but it *does* have… interpretations.”

Elias reached into his pocket and pulled out a small data chip. “I’ve already documented the rationale,” he said, holding it up between them. The Archivists behind Darius tensed.

“You’re playing a dangerous game,” one of the Archivists murmured. Her nameplate read *Liora*, and her eyes were sharp with something like respect.

Elias ignored the warning. “I’m playing *corporate*.”

Darius’s smirk faded, replaced by something colder. “Then let the game begin.” He snapped his fingers.

The Atrium’s lights flickered, and for a moment, Elias saw the future—one where his decision either saved everything or destroyed it all. The choice was made.

Now, he had to live with the consequences.

**Synergy-pact clause:** *By blood and balance, this act is sealed—may the Ledger’s Echo judge it true.*
