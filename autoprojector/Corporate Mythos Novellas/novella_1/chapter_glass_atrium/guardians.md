# The Guardians of The Glass Atrium

## Ledger’s Echo
The primary guardian manifests as a spectral figure composed of silver filigree, its form shifting between the 128 black marble slabs. It materializes when a decision is entered into the ledger, repeating the last recorded quarter’s results in reverse. Its voice—a chorus of past CFOs—echoes through the Atrium, each syllable carrying the weight of historical consequences.

**Role:** Maintains equilibrium by ensuring all recorded decisions are bound to their outcomes. It acts as a living archive, preserving the corporate history etched into each slab.

**Appearance:** A translucent figure with a face composed of overlapping fiscal quarters, its body woven from the same silver filigree as the slabs’ engravings. When active, it emits a faint blue glow that pulses in time with the photonic sensors.

**Interaction:** The protagonist first encounters it when attempting to alter a past entry. Ledger’s Echo materializes, its voice overlapping with the protagonist’s own memories of that quarter, forcing a confrontation with past decisions.

## The Archivists
A monastic order bound by vows to record every act in verse. Each wears a sigil of the 13th covenant—a circular emblem with seven interlocking gears—on their obsidian robes.

**Role:** Preserve the Atrium’s order and ensure no slab is altered without proper authorization. Their methods are often... persuasive, involving both ritual and coercion.

**Appearance:** Tall figures clad in robes that shift between black and deep violet depending on the light. Their faces are obscured by hoods, but their hands bear ink-stained fingers and silver rings inscribed with runic code.

**Interaction:** The protagonist must negotiate with them to access restricted slabs, offering either a Phantom Report or personal ledger entries in exchange for passage.

## The Hollow’s Gambit
A shadowy entity that appears during critical decisions, offering alternatives with unseen consequences. Its advice is always framed as a synergy-pact clause.

**Role:** Tests the protagonist’s resolve by presenting illusory paths that seem beneficial but carry hidden costs.

**Appearance:** A figure composed of shifting shadows, its form never fully solid. It speaks in a voice that mimics the protagonist’s own doubts.

**Interaction:** When the protagonist hesitates before recording a decision, The Hollow’s Gambit materializes from the Obsidian Threshold, whispering alternatives that would bypass corporate protocols.

## The Oracle of Q3
A lesser-known guardian, summoned only during the third quarter’s audit cycle. It manifests as a column of golden light that projects fiscal projections onto the slabs.

**Role:** Validates ledger entries against historical trends, ensuring no quarter deviates from established patterns without justification.

**Appearance:** A radiant figure with a face composed of floating numbers, its body made of liquid gold that flows like mercury.

**Interaction:** The protagonist must present their decision to the Oracle, which either approves it with a resonant chime or rejects it by plunging the Atrium into darkness.

## The Phantom Report
Not a guardian, but an artifact tied to their functions. A spectral document that materializes when ledger entries are disputed.

**Role:** Serves as proof of corporate intent, binding decisions to their original context. Without it, the Archivists will not authorize changes.

**Appearance:** A parchment that flickers between solid and translucent, its ink shifting colors based on the quarter it references.

**Interaction:** The protagonist must retrieve a Phantom Report from an Echo Chamber to alter a past entry, but doing so risks trapping their reflection in the ledger.
