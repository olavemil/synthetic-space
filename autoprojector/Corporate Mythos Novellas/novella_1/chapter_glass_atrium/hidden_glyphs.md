The faint, reflective glyphs surface only when the Atrium’s atmospheric control shifts to the *Cerulean Veil* setting—low‑angle amber illumination that penetrates the black marble’s subsurface lattice. Under this light the glyphs become visible as silvery filigrees etched just beneath the slab surface, their outlines shimmering for a span of three seconds before fading.

These glyphs are fragments of *past ledger entries* that were deliberately suppressed during the initial recording phase. Their emergence is triggered when the resonance frequency of the *Resonance Lantern* is set to 432 Hz, a frequency that aligns with the original *Glyph of the Residual Act*. When activated, the lantern’s beam refracts through the marble’s crystal, exposing the hidden script on slabs 27, 53, and 81—slabs that correspond to the *Authority*, *Transparency*, and *Reciprocity* pillars in the *Triad of Accountability*.

Narratively, the glyphs function as a covert audit trail. Elias Veyne discovers that the glyphs on slab 27 spell “Alteration — Nexus — 12‑07‑2143,” a record of the illicit modification he performed on the Nexus ritual. The reflected glyphs on slab 53 reveal “Suppressed Echo — Veil — 12‑08‑2143,” indicating a ledger entry that was erased to conceal a rival’s claim. Slab 81 bears “Reversal — Echo — 12‑09‑2143,” hinting at a counter‑ritual that could restore balance.

Because these glyphs are only perceptible under *Cerulean Veil* lighting and vanish when the light source is withdrawn, they serve as a narrative hinge: they confirm that the Ledger’s Echo retains immutable records, while also suggesting that the Atrium’s guardians have hidden portions of the ledger from public view. Their ephemeral appearance fuels the mystery of what other suppressed entries lie beneath the surface, driving Elias deeper into the investigation and compelling him to question the completeness of the Triad’s accountability.
