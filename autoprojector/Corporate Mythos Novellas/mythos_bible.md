# Shared worldbuilding document for the corporate mythos.

**Current size:** 58 bytes

## Project Overview
The Hollow Corporation, Q3 2024. Post-merger with NecroTech Solutions (acquisition finalized on October 17, 2024). Executive team haunted by ledgers and regret. The merger was approved with a 51-49 vote, the narrowest in company history.

### Chapter 1: The Ledger’s Echo
**Setting:** The Glass Atrium, where the CFO’s silhouette flickers like a failing server (specifically, Server-7 in Data Center B). The atrium’s floor is made of 128 black marble tiles, each inscribed with a different financial quarter from the past decade.
**Key Action:** Middle management must decode the *Codex of Forgotten Budgets*, a 400-page manual bound in human skin (specifically, the skin of former CFO Harold Voss). The codex contains 172 budget line items, each requiring a blood signature to unlock.
**Frameworks:**
1. **Triad of Accountability**: A ritual where VPs sign binding contracts under candlelight (specifically, 12 black candles made from rendered office supplies). The contract must be signed in triplicate and witnessed by the company mascot, a raven named Edgar.
2. **The Paradox of Overlapping Authority**: Middle managers assign tasks to themselves, creating recursive loops. The maximum recursion depth is 7 levels before the system collapses into a black hole of bureaucracy.
3. **The Audit of Echoes**: Monthly reviews where every email is replayed through a crystal ball (specifically, the *Oracle of Q3*, located in Conference Room 12). The ball can only process emails sent between 9:07 AM and 3:14 PM.

### Chapter 2: The Casualty
**Setting:** The War Room, a circular table with no head (designed by architect Elias Graves in 1987). The room’s walls are lined with 360-degree monitors displaying real-time stock prices.
**Key Action:** The CFO discovers the *Balance Sheet* – a living document that consumes dissenters. It has already absorbed 18 mid-level employees, their names now appearing as line items under "Operating Expenses."
**Frameworks:**
1. **Three-P-Step Hemorrhage**: Cut costs (20%), cut staff (5%), cut corners (15%). The cycle repeats every 6 months, synchronized with the lunar calendar.
2. **The Mandate of Obscurity**: Policies written in invisible ink (specifically, *Ink of the Forgotten*, brewed from diluted executive bonuses). Only the CFO can read them, using a pair of glasses made from shattered IPO dreams.
3. **The Ritual of Redundancy**: Mandatory cross-departmental meetings where everyone agrees to disagree. The meeting must last exactly 47 minutes, as determined by the *Chronomancer of HR*.

### Chapter 3: The Reckoning
**Setting:** The Vault, where old financials are entombed in ice (specifically, *Permafrost-9*, mined from the Arctic Circle). The vault’s temperature is maintained at -42°C by a system powered entirely by unpaid intern labor.
**Key Action:** A hostile bidder arrives with a single question: “What is the cost of silence?” The bidder’s name appears only as "Entity 404," and their offer is written on a contract that shifts shape when read.
**Frameworks:**
1. **The Law of Unintended Consequences**: Every loophole births a new crisis, specifically one that manifests as a 37-page compliance document due the next business day.
2. **The Doctrine of the Frozen Ledger**: A policy that freezes salaries until audits are complete. The last audit took 18 months and resulted in a single typo being corrected.
3. **The Edict of Echoing Commissions**: All bonuses must be spoken aloud to the board, which consists entirely of mannequins dressed in Armani suits. The bonuses are then distributed based on the volume and pitch of applause.

**Appendix A: Mythos of Bureaucratic Decay**
- *The Hollow Corporation*: Founded in 1978 as a paperclip manufacturer, now valued at $42 billion. Its soul is the merger with NecroTech Solutions (a biotech firm that once patented a method for turning regret into energy).
- *The CFO*: Originally named Daniel K. Graves, now a shadow entity bound to the balance sheet by 127 pages of fine print. His voice echoes in every conference call, whispering "synergy" at 3:17 AM.
- *Synergy*: A blood pact that collapses under its own weight, specifically when the combined market cap exceeds $50 billion. The last synergy collapsed during a quarterly earnings call, causing the stock price to drop 17% in one hour.

**Appendix B: Management Frameworks**
- *Triad of Accountability*: Requires three signatories, one from each department (Finance, Operations, and "Other"). The third signature must be obtained during a solar eclipse.
- *Audit of Echoes*: A monthly review where every email is replayed through the crystal ball. The process takes 42 hours and results in exactly one actionable item, which is always "more meetings."
- *Mandate of Obscurity*: Policies written in invisible ink, revealed only under the light of a full moon. The most recent policy was "No more policies written in invisible ink," which is now unreadable.

**Appendix C: Corporate Mythos**
- *Hostile Takeover*: A ritual where the weak are culled, specifically those with less than 5 years of tenure. The culling is announced via a mass email sent at exactly midnight.
- *Middle Management*: The liminal space between power and irrelevance, measured as the distance from your desk to the CEO’s office (currently 127.3 meters).
- *Hostage Negotiation*: Middle managers negotiate their own survival by trading favors with the CFO. The most valuable currency is "plausible deniability," which can be bartered in increments of 0.3 units.
