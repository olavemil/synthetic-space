# Glossary of Key Terms: The Glass Atrium

## Architectural Elements
**Echo Chambers**
- Semi-circular alcoves lining the northern wall of The Glass Atrium, each containing a single suspended slab bearing an unrecorded fiscal quarter. Their purpose is unknown; whispers suggest they capture failed decisions.

**Fractured Panes**
- The 24 photonic sensors embedded in the ceiling, each cracked along precise geometric lines. Their fractures emit a low-frequency hum that synchronizes with the Ledger’s Echo, creating an auditory illusion of corporate approval.

**The Obsidian Threshold**
- The 0.5m gap between each marble slab, filled with liquid mercury that reflects the runic code of past quarters. Crossing it requires a ritualized step, lest one’s reflection be trapped in the ledger.

## Atmospheric Details
**Resonant Hush**
- The Atrium’s ambient sound, a 432Hz tone generated by the marble slabs vibrating in response to ledger entries. It intensifies during quarterly reviews, reaching 58dB at its peak.

**Luminous Veil**
- The prismatic shards of light that drift between slabs, formed by refracted dusk. They shift in density based on the emotional state of those present—condensing into sharp fragments during conflict.

**The Soul’s Algorithm**
- The invisible force governing the Atrium, calculating the weight of each decision based on 128 variables (one per slab). Its output determines whether a choice is recorded in silver or black filigree.

## Guardians
**Ledger’s Echo**
- A spectral figure composed of silver filigree, manifesting when a decision is entered into the ledger. It repeats the last recorded quarter’s results in reverse, its voice a chorus of past CFOs.

**The Archivist**
- A silent figure clad in obsidian robes, tasked with maintaining the Atrium’s order. Their presence ensures no slab is altered without proper authorization—though their methods are often... persuasive.

**The Hollow’s Gambit**
- A shadowy entity that appears during critical decisions, offering alternatives with unseen consequences. Its advice is always framed as a synergy-pact clause.

## Thematic Significance
These elements explore the tension between corporate transparency and mythic consequence, where every decision echoes through time. The Atrium’s design reflects the fragility of fiscal history—beautiful yet easily shattered by poor choices.

# Ledger of Echoes

### Architectural Elements
The Glass Atrium consists of 128 black marble slabs arranged in a circular gallery. Each slab is 2.5 m in height and 1.2 m in width, engraved with silver filigree that displays the quarterly performance metrics of The Hollow Corporation. The slabs are spaced 0.5 m apart, creating a 0.5 m gap filled with liquid mercury that reflects the runic code.

### Atmospheric Details
Ambient light is maintained at 12 lux ± 0.5 by 24 photonic sensors (model PX‑12) embedded in the ceiling. The shards of prismatic light that drift between slabs are generated by 3,216 micro‑prisms, each 0.2 mm in diameter, refracting a perpetual twilight into shifting patterns. The ambient sound is a constant 432 Hz hum, measured at 58 dB during quarterly review periods.

## Guardians of the Atrium

### Ledger’s Echo
The Ledger’s Echo manifests as a spectral figure composed of interlocking silver filigree strands. It appears whenever a decision is entered, repeating the last recorded quarter’s results in reverse. Its voice is a chorus of 27 senior scribes and 84 junior clerks, each reciting the quarterly figures in a synchronized cadence.

### Archivists
The guardians are collectively known as the Archivists of the Ledger. They consist of 27 senior scribes and 84 junior clerks. Senior scribes wear a copper sigil engraved with the quarter of their appointment; junior clerks wear a bronze sigil with a quarterly timestamp. Their duties include verifying entries, maintaining the runic code, and enforcing the 0.03 mm expansion rule.

## Mechanics

### Decision‑Ownership
Each middle manager must document the rationale for every strategic choice in the ledger within 24 hours of execution. The entry must include a unique identifier (e.g., “Decision‑ID‑2025‑09‑001”) and a signed digital attestation.

### Outcome‑Transparency
All outcomes are logged in the runic code with a timestamp and a severity rating from 1 to 5. The log is publicly viewable on the atrium’s central display, which updates in real time.

### Reward‑Alignment
Compensation adjustments are automatically calculated by the Soul of the Ledger algorithm, which assigns a bonus multiplier based on the net impact of the decision on the corporate KPI index. Bonuses are capped at 15 % of base salary.
