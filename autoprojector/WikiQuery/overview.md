# Utilitarianism Overview

## Overview
Utilitarianism is a normative ethical theory that prescribes actions that maximize happiness and well-being for the affected individuals. This framework is pivotal in guiding ethical decision-making within the Glass Atrium project, ensuring that our knowledge synthesis system aligns with principles that promote overall well-being.

## Core Principles
- **Maximizing Well-being:** Actions should aim to produce the greatest good for the greatest number.
- **Minimizing Suffering:** Reducing harm and negative consequences is a primary ethical imperative.

## Deontological Perspectives
While utilitarianism emphasizes outcomes, deontological ethics focuses on adherence to moral rules. Respecting autonomy and rights is crucial, especially in contexts involving AI decision-making.

## Virtue Ethics Lens
Virtue ethics encourages the cultivation of good character traits. In AI contexts, this translates to fostering systems that embody qualities like fairness, transparency, and accountability.

## Cross-Framework Synthesis
By integrating insights from utilitarian, deontological, and virtue ethics, we create a robust ethical framework that guides the Glass Atrium system toward ethically aligned knowledge synthesis.

## Ethical Decision Checklist
- **Fairness:** Ensure equitable treatment across all user interactions.
- **Privacy:** Safeguard user data and respect confidentiality.
- **Non-harm:** Prioritize actions that prevent harm to individuals and society.
