# Ethical Air-Agent System: Core Objectives and Utilitarian Framework

## 1. Ethical Objective Definition
The primary ethical objective of an ethical air‑agent system is to **maximize overall wellbeing** while **minimizing harm** through decisions informed by utilitarian principles.

## 2. Core Goals
1. **Promote Well‑Being:** Prioritize actions that enhance health, safety, and flourishing for all stakeholders.
2. **Minimize Suffering:** Reduce physical, psychological, and social harms that arise from both normal operation and failure modes.
3. **Ensure Fair Distribution:** Allocate benefits and burdens equitably across users, communities, and future generations.

## 3. Relevant AI Mechanisms
- **Outcome Prediction:** Use predictive models to estimate consequences of actions on wellbeing metrics.
- **Preference Elicitation:** Capture and model stakeholder values to align system behavior with wellbeing goals.
- **Uncertainty Quantification:** Estimate confidence in predictions to support robust, risk‑aware decisions.
- **Multi‑Objective Optimization:** Balance wellbeing, efficiency, and fairness when selecting among competing actions.

## 4. Impact Assessment
- **Well‑Being Metrics:** Define quantitative indicators (e.g., QALYs, safety incidents) to monitor outcomes.
- **Harm Monitoring:** Implement continuous surveillance for adverse events and near‑misses.
- **Equity Audits:** Regularly evaluate distributional effects to detect and correct disparities.

## 5. Decision‑Making Checklist (Ethical Priorities)
- [ ] Fairness: Ensure equitable treatment across demographic and socio‑economic groups.
- [ ] Non‑Harm: Prevent actions that could cause physical or psychological injury.
- [ ] Transparency: Provide clear rationales for decisions, especially when outcomes are uncertain.

## 6. Executive Summary (Executive Summary)
This document outlines a framework for building ethical air‑agent systems grounded in utilitarian ethics. Core goals focus on maximizing wellbeing, minimizing harm, and ensuring fair distribution. By integrating outcome prediction, preference modeling, and rigorous impact assessment, the system can make context‑aware decisions that respect both human values and safety imperatives.

## 7. Decision‑Making Matrix
| Criterion | High Priority | Medium Priority | Low Priority |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Safety** | Must avoid any action risking injury. | Monitor for minor operational anomalies. | Track non‑essential incidents. |
| **Well‑Being** | Prioritize actions that improve health outcomes. | Support routine operational improvements. | Encourage optional wellness features. |
| **Fairness** | Guarantee equal access to services. | Promote equitable resource allocation. | Observe minor disparities. |

## 8. Glossary
- **Utilitarian‑LLM Balance:** The practice of calibrating AI recommendations to reflect both outcome maximization and respect for individual rights, ensuring that aggregate benefits are not achieved at the expense of fundamental ethical constraints.
