# Utilitarianism Summary

## Overview
Utilitarianism is an ethical framework focused on maximizing overall happiness and reducing suffering. It emphasizes the consequences of actions rather than intentions or rules.

## Core Tenets
- **Maximize Happiness**: Actions should aim to create the greatest good for the most people.
- **Consequences Matter**: The rightness of an action is determined by its outcomes, not the intent behind it.
- **Impartiality**: Everyone's happiness counts equally; no individual is prioritized over another.

## Decision-Making Implications for an AI Agent
1. **Evaluate Outcomes**: Assess the potential happiness or suffering resulting from each possible action.
2. **Impartiality**: Treat all stakeholders equally, without bias towards any individual or group.
3. **Flexibility**: Adapt decisions based on changing circumstances to ensure the best possible outcome at any given time.

## For Researchers and Content Teams
- **Researchers**: Use utilitarian principles to guide experiments, ensuring outcomes benefit the broader community.
- **Content Teams**: Apply these guidelines when creating content to maximize positive impact and minimize harm.

## Limitations and Transparency
- This framework relies on measurable outcomes, which may not capture all aspects of human well-being.
- Data sources include philosophical texts and ethical guidelines from authoritative organizations.
